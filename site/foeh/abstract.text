The future of effects in Haskell
================================

Haskell provides rich abstractions for augmenting code with user
defined effects, notably monads and monad transformers. However, the
standard abstractions suffer from various shortcomings relating to
readability, modularity, and compositionality. Recently, a number of
researchers have explored alternative approaches to effectful
programming that aim to address such shortcomings. The goal of this
panel is to discuss the merits and drawbacks of these alternative
approaches, and how best to incorporate them into Haskell practice.

The panel will comprise of the following:

  * [Oleg Kiselyov](http://okmij.org/ftp/), Monterey, USA ([extensible effects](http://www.cs.indiana.edu/~sabry/papers/exteff.pdf), [observable laziness](http://okmij.org/ftp/Haskell/index.html#lazyIO-not-True)).
  * [Daan Leijen](http://research.microsoft.com/en-us/people/daan/), Microsoft Research Redmond, USA ([strict monadic programming](http://www.cs.umd.edu/~mwh/papers/swamy11monad.html), [effect row-polymorphism](http://research.microsoft.com/apps/pubs/default.aspx?id=200436)).
  * [Tom Schrijvers](http://users.ugent.be/~tschrijv/), Ghent University, Belgium ([monad zippers](http://ropas.snu.ac.kr/~bruno/papers/VirtualMonads.pdf), [incremental programming](http://users.ugent.be/~tschrijv/Research/papers/jfp_mri.pdf)).
  * [Tarmo Uustalu](http://cs.ioc.ee/~tarmo/), Tallinn University, Estonia ([relative monads](http://www.cs.nott.ac.uk/~txa/publ/jrelmon.pdf), [comonads](http://www.ioc.ee/~tarmo/papers/cmcs08.pdf)).
  * [Philip Wadler](http://homepages.inf.ed.ac.uk/wadler/), the University of Edinburgh, Scotland ([monads](http://homepages.inf.ed.ac.uk/wadler/papers/imperative/imperative.ps), [type-classes](http://homepages.inf.ed.ac.uk/wadler/papers/classhask/classhask.ps)).

Session Chair: [Sam Lindley](http://homepages.inf.ed.ac.uk/slindley/), the University of Edinburgh, Scotland ([effect handlers](http://homepages.inf.ed.ac.uk/slindley/papers/handlers.pdf), [effect row-polymorphism](http://homepages.inf.ed.ac.uk/slindley/papers/corelinks.pdf)).
